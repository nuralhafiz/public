INTRODUCTION  
 
1.1 PROJECT  BACKGROUND  
 
Manual administrative processes and inefficient record -keeping have long been significant 
challenges in managing student accommodation at the German -Malaysian Institute (GMI). 
Many students experience delays during room registration, struggle with lost application 
forms, and face difficulties tracking maintenance requests. These issues highlight the urgent 
need for a more st reamlined, transparent, and digital approach to hostel management that 
benefits both students and administrative staff.  
 
 
 
To address these challenges, this project introduces GMI HostelKu, a web -based hostel 
management system designed to automate and cent ralize all accommodation -related 
operations. The system serves as a unified platform, allowing students to submit room 
applications, report maintenance issues, and track their requests in real -time. By replacing 
paper -based workflows with digital processes , GMI HostelKu minimizes manual errors, 
reduces processing delays, and enhances the overall user experience for both students and 
staff.  
 
 
 
GMI HostelKu is  designed  to provide  a user -friendly and efficient  solution  tailored specifically 
to the needs  of GMI’s hostel  ecosystem.  Key features  include  online  application  and approval 
workflows, real -time room availability updates, automated notifications, and a dedicated 
maintenance  reporting  portal.  This project  aims  to ensure  that the system  is not only functional 
but also accessible  and easy  to use, thereby  improving  operational  efficiency  and satisfaction 
across all user groups.  
Furthermore, the system emphasizes transparency and accountability by providing live 
updates and centralized access to informa tion. Administrators can monitor occupancy rates, 
track  maintenance  progress,  and generate  reports  with ease,  while  students  gain visibility  into 
their application  status  and request  history.  By digitizing  these  core processes,  GMI HostelKu 
reduces  administrative  workload,  encourages  timely  communication,  and supports  data-driven 
decision -making.

3  1.2 PROBLEM  STATEMENT  
 
 
 
1. Long  queues  and persistent  delays  are a regular  issue  due to slow,  manual  processing.  
 
 
Picture students lining up down the hallway, just to submit a single form. This is a common 
scene  during  busy  periods,  forcing  everyone  to waste  precious  time on simple  administrative 
tasks. The slow, paper -based system creates unnecessary waiting for students and a 
constant backlog of work for staff.  
 
 
2. The manual  record -keeping  system  is highly  vulner able to misplaced  forms  and human 
error.  
 
 
A student's application can easily get lost in a pile of papers, or a simple mistake can be 
made when copying details by hand. When this happens, it is incredibly difficult to track 
down  the correct  information.  This lack of dependable  data compromises  the reliability  and 
integrity of vital student and occupancy records, leading to confusion and mistrust.  
 
 
3. Hostel  operations  lack real-time data or transparency  in key management  areas.  
 
 
Is a room available? How long will a repair take? Nobody can get an immediate answer. 
Wardens  cannot  make  fast decisions, and  students  are often  left in the dark about  the status 
of their requests. This absence of live information prevents quick, informed decision -making 
and strains communication between everyone involved.

4  1.3 PROJECT  OBJECTIVES  
 
 
 
1. To develop  a web-based  platform  called  “GMI  HostelKu”  
 
 
Imagine a single website where students can handle everything to do with their hostel life, 
and where staff have all the information they need at their fingertips. This platform is 
designed  to be that one-stop-shop,  making  hostel  management  straightforward  and hassle - 
free for everyone involved.  
 
 
2. To digitalize the hostel registration process for eac h new semester.  
 
 
We're  saying  goodbye  to long lines and paper  forms.  Instead, students  will be able to apply 
for their room  from anywhere,  at any time, and  get clear  updates on  their application status 
every step of the way. This makes securing a room a m uch simpler and less stressful 
experience.  
 
 
3. To facilitate maintenance issue reporting and notification  
 
 
If a student reports a leaky tap or a broken light, the right staff member will be notified 
instantly.  This means  problems  can be assigned  and fixed  much  faster,  creating  a safer  and 
more comfortable living environment for students without unnecessary delays.

5  1.4 PROJECT  SCOPE  
 
 
 
TYPE  OF SYSTEM  
GMI HostelKu  is a web-based  platform  designed  to bring  hostel  management into  the digital  
age. Think of it as a central nervous system for all accommodation operations at GMI - 
replacing  paper  forms  and manual  processes with  a streamlined, online  solution  that makes 
managing hostel life smoother for everyone involved.  
 
 
TARGETED  USER  
 
 
GMI HostelKu  is built with two main  users  in mind:  
Students living in GMI hostels will use the system to handle everything from applying for a 
room to reporting maintenance issues. Instead of waiting in lines or filling out paper forms, 
they can manage  their hostel  needs  through  their phone  or computer,  getting  instant  updates 
about their applications and requests.  
Hostel Administrators and Wardens will use the system's dashboard to manage the entire 
accommodation  process.  They  can quickly  see which  rooms  are available,  process  student 
applications, and track maintenance jobs. All from one central location, saving time and 
reducing paperwork.

2.1 FEASIBILITY  STUDIES  
 
 
To further assess the feasibility of the  GMi HostelKu  system, a  student feedback survey  was 
executed  to understand  their experiences  and expectations  regarding  hostel  registration.  The 
survey aimed to  identify issues with the existing manual registration process and evaluate 
students’ readiness for an online system.  
A total of 51 students from the German -Malaysian Institute participated in the survey 
conducted to evaluate the current hostel registration process and gather feedback regarding 
the proposed HostelKu digital system.

Figure  2.1.1:  Questionnaire  1 
 
1. Efficiency  of the Current  Manual  Hostel  Registration  Process  
 
Based  on the responses,  
 
• 45.1%  of students  rated  the current  manual  registration  process  as slow,  
• 35.3%  found  it very inefficient,  
• 11.8%  remained  neutral,  and 
• Only a small  percentage  found  it efficient  or very efficient.  
 
 
This indicates that a majority of students experience delays, long queues, and processing 
issues under the current manual system. The result highlights a strong need for digital 
transformation to improve efficiency and convenience in the hostel registration workflow.

2. Main  Issues  Faced  During  Registration  
 
The survey results show several key challenges in the current manual hostel registration 
process:  
• Long  Queues  (96.1%)  – Most  students  experience  long waiting  times,  especially 
during peak registration periods. Eg, semester registration.  
• Lack of Clear Commu nication (78.4%)  – Students often face uncertainty regarding  
room allocation and approval updates.  
• Repeated Data Entry Errors (64.7%) – Manual form filling leads to errors and delays 
in processing applications.  
• Limited  Staff Response  (2%)  – A few responden ts noted  that staff were  overwhelmed, 
causing slower assistance.  
 
 
Overall, these findings highlight that the manual process is inefficient and time -consuming, 
supporting the need for a digital system like HostelKu to improve speed, accuracy, and 
communication.

This overwhelmingly positive response demonstrates that almost all students support the 
implementation of an online hostel registration system. It confirms that students are 
comf ortable  using  digital  platforms  and view them  as a practical  solution  to existing  problems 
in manual process

key expectations for the system.

3.1 SOFTWARE  REQUIREMENT  SPECIFICATION  
 
3.1.1  SDLC  PROCESS  
 
 
 
Figure  3.1.1.1:  SDLC  Process  
The development of the GMI HostelKu system followed the  Agile methodology, which we 
selected for its iterative, user -focused nature. This approach allowed us to break down the 
project  into short  development  cycles  rather  than attempting  to build  the entire  system  at once. 
After each cycle, we reviewed a functional piece of the software with our stakeholde rs, 
wardens and students, gathering their feedback. This continuous feedback loop was crucial, 
enabling  us to refine  features  and priorities  to ensure  the final product  truly addressed  the real- 
world problems of administrative delays and manual processes  
===PAGE===
19  1. Planning  Phase  
 
 
During  the planning  phase,  the following  objectives  were  defined:  
 
 
Main  Goal:  
 
 
Develop  a reliable,  efficient,  and user-friendly  web-based  hostel  management  system  to 
digitalize accommodation operations at GMI.  
 
 
Key Stakeholders:  
 
 
Students, hostel administrators/wardens, and the IT support team. The initial product 
backlog was created, listing major tasks such as user authentication, room application 
processing,  maintenance  reporting,  real-time occupancy  monitoring,  and administrative 
dashboard features.  
===PAGE===
20  2. Requirements  and Analysis  Phase  
 
 
The system's  requirements  were  gathered  through  stakeholder  interviews  and 
document analysis, and were refined iteratively. They consisted of:  
 
 
Functional  Requirements:  
 
 
• Secure  login  and role-based  access  for students  and administrators.  
• Online  room  application  and automated  allocation  system.  
• Real-time maintenance  reporting  and tracking  system.  
• Room  availability  monitoring  and occupancy  management.  
 
 
Non-Functional  Requirements:  
 
 
• High system  availability  during  critical  enrollment  periods.  
• Responsive  design  accessible  on both desktop  and mobile  devices.  
• Intuitive  interface  requiring  minimal  training  for users.  
 
 
Technical  Analysis:  
 
 
We built the GMI HostelKu  system  using  PHP,  a powerful  and reliable  programming  language 
that works behind the scenes to run all the important logic, like processing applications and 
managing  user logins.  All the information,  from student  details  to room  records,  is safely  stored 
and organized in  a MySQL database, which acts  as the system's secure  digital  filing cabinet.  
 
 
 
To make  sure the system  is easy  for everyone  to use, we designed  the interface 
with Bootstrap. This means whether a  student is checking room availability on  their phone or 
a warden  is managing  applications  on a desktop  computer,  the website  will look  great and  be 
simple to navigate on any device.  
===PAGE===
21  3. Design  Phase  
 
 
The syste m's design evolved organically through each sprint, shaped by ongoing feedback 
from our stakeholders.  We focused  on creating  intuitive  workflows  that would  feel natural  for 
both students and hostel staff.  
 
 
Process  Flowcharts : We mapped  out every key  process,  from how a student  applies  for a 
room to how a warden resolves a maintenance ticket, ensuring no step was overlooked.  
 
 
Use Case Diagrams:  These diagrams helped us visualize all the ways students and 
administrators  would  interact  with the system,  clarifying  responsibilities  for each  user role 
from the very beginning.  
 
 
 
 
UI/UX  Wireframes : Using  Figma,  we built clickable  prototypes  of the student  portal  and 
admin dashboard. Our design goal was simplicity and clarity, making sure that tasks  
like submitting  a room  application  or checking  occupancy  rates  would  be straightforward, 
even for first -time users.  
 
 
These designs weren't set in stone; they were living documents. After each sprint, we 
reviewed  the wireframes  and flowcharts  with our stakeholders,  refining  them  based  on their 
hands -on experience and suggestions.  
===PAGE===
22  4. Implementation  Phase  
 
 
We brought  the GMI HostelKu  system  to life through  a series  of focused,  two-week  sprints. 
Each sprint delivered a concrete, working part of the platform, building momentum and 
allowing for continuous improvement.  
 
 
Sprint  1: Laying  the Foundation  
We started by building the core gateway to the system: a secure user registration and login 
system.  This established  the crucial  separation  between  student  and admin  access  from day 
one. 
 
 
Sprint  2: Digitalizing  the Core  Service  
Our focus  shifted  to the heart  of the problem, the  manual  application  process.  We developed 
the complete online room application and allocation system, allowing students to apply for a 
room and administrators to manage those applications digitally.  
 
 
 
 
Sprint  3: Enabling  Real -Time  Reporting  
We built the maintenance  reporting  module,  empowering  students  to log facility  issues  with a 
few clicks and giving staff a centralized dashboard to track and manage these requests, 
replacing lost paper forms with instant digital tickets.  
 
 
Sprint  4: Creating  Visibility  and Insight  
We implemented  the real-time monitoring  features,  including  the room  availability dashboard 
and administrative overview panels. This provided the transparency that was missing in the 
old system, allowing for data -driven decisions.  
 
 
 
 
Sprint  5: Automating  and Finalizing  
In the final development sprint, we automated system notifications and built the report 
generation  tool, enabling  wardens  to create  occupancy  and maintenance  reports  with a 
single click, wrapping up the core administrative functionality.  
===PAGE===
23  5. Testing  Phase  
 
 
Testing wasn't just a final st ep, it was a constant companion throughout the entire 
development  journey.  We believed  in catching  issues  early  and often  to build  a system 
everyone could rely on.  
 
 
I. Unit Testing:  We tested  every  individual  piece  of the puzzle  in isolation,  like the room 
allocation algorithm or the login verification function, to ensure each one worked 
perfectly on its own.  
 
 
II. Integration Testing:  Once  individual parts  were  ready, we  checked  how they worked 
together. We made sure that submitting a room application corr ectly updated the 
room  availability  and that a new maintenance  ticket  instantly  appeared  on the admin 
dashboard.  
 
 
III. System Testing:  We put the entire, fully -assembled system through its paces, 
simulating  a full semester  of student  registrations  and maintenance  reports  to ensure 
it could handle real -world pressure.  
 
 
IV. User Acceptance Testing (UAT):  This was the most crucial test. We invited our key 
stakeholders,  including  hostel  wardens  and students,  to use the system  themselves. 
Their real -world feedb ack on everything from button placement to form clarity led to 
immediate, practical improvements, which made the system truly theirs.  
 
 
Whenever  a test uncovered  a bug or a usability  hiccup, we  logged  it, prioritized  it, and made 
sure it was fixed in the v ery next development cycle.  
===PAGE===
24  6. Deployment  Phase  
 
 
We introduced  the GMI HostelKu  system  to the campus  community  in a careful,  staged 
rollout to ensure a smooth and secure transition from the old paper -based methods.  
 
 
 
Staged  Rollout:  After  each  successful  sprint,  the new features  were  deployed  to a private 
staging server. This gave the development team and a small group of testers a safe 
environment to validate everything before it went live.  
 
 
 
Go-Live with Confidence:  Once  the complete  system  was thoroughly  tested  and approved  in 
the staging environment, we scheduled the final deployment to the live production server.  
We planned  this outside  of peak  hours  to ensure  minimal  disruption  to hostel  operations.  
 
 
 
Safety  First:  We prepared  detailed  deployment  checklists  and rollback  plans.  This meant  that 
if any unexpected issue arose during the live update, we could quickly revert to  
the previous  stable  version,  guaranteeing  that hostel  operations  would  never  be 
negativel y impacted.

An interview was conducted with a key stakeholder, Mr. Mohd Saimi Mohd Zain, the Hostel 
Warden  at the German -Malaysian  Institute  (GMi),  who is directly  involved  in managing  student 
hostel  applications,  room  allocations,  and maintenance  reporting.  He shared  valuable  insights

entry  errors,  paperwork  overload,  and long processing  times  during  peak  registration  periods.  
According to Mr. Saimi, the manual process often causes delays in student check -ins, 
miscommunication about room assignments, and difficulties tracking maintenance reports. 
These challenges highlight the n eed for a centralized, automated system to handle hostel 
management tasks efficiently.  
When presented with the proposed GMi HostelKu system, the stakeholder expressed strong 
support for the platform’s concept. He emphasized that features such as online reg istration, 
QR code verification, and real -time application tracking would greatly improve transparency 
and streamline  daily hostel  operations.  The integration  of a maintenance  reporting  feature  was 
also praised for promoting faster communication between st udents and hostel staff.  
Mr. Saimi  also recommended  ensuring  data security  and reliable  user authentication  to protect 
student records and maintain system integrity. From an administrative perspective, he 
believed  the system  could  significantly  reduce  workload,  eliminate  redundant  paperwork,  and 
improve overall efficiency.  
Furthermore, survey data collected from students supported  these findings — with over 90% 
expressing interest in using a digital hostel registration platform. Thi s demonstrates clear 
demand and readiness among users for a web -based system.  
Overall, the results confirm that the HostelKu system addresses real operational pain points 
and provides practical value to both students and hostel management. With continuous 
improvement, the platform can serve as a reliable and scalable digital solution for campus 
accommodation management.

6.1 CONCLUSION  
 
 
In conclusion, the current manual and paper -based system for managing student 
accommodation at the German -Malaysian Institute (GMI) is plagued by inefficiencies that 
result in significant administrative burdens, data inaccuracies, and poor communication. The 
identified  problems,  including  slow processing  times,  vulnerable  record -keeping,  and a lack of 
real-time operational visibility directly hinder  the quality of service  for both  staff and  students.

aims to develop a centralised, web -based platform that will digitalise and streamline all core 
hostel management processes. By achieving its objectives automating the registration and 
room allocation workflow, impl ementing an instant notification system for maintenance, and 
providing a user -friendly digital interface the  GMi HostelKu system is poised to transform the 
hostel management experience.  
 
 
 
Ultimately,  this system  will eliminate  the delays  and errors  of the old method,  provide  real-time 
data transparency, and enable informed, efficient decision -making. The successful 
implementation  of GMi HostelKu  will mark  a critical  upgrade,  close  the service  quality  gap and 
creating a faster, smarter, and more reliable hostel management environment for the entire 
GMi community.

101  Interview  Questions  with Hostel  Warden  
1. What's  the most  difficult  part of managing  the hostel  right now?  
 
 
Mostly  the paperwork.  Forms  get lost so easily, especially  during  busy  periods. The  long 
queues  and slow processing  create  frustration  for everyone,  my staff and students  alike.  
 
 
2. How long does  it take to process  room  applications  and maintenance  issues?  
 
 
Much longer than it should. Processing applications can take days because we have to 
check  everything  manually. For  maintenance  issues,  it sometimes  takes  a full day just to 
assign someone before the repair even begins.  
 
 
3. How do you know  which  rooms  are available  right now?  
 
 
We use a whiteboard and a record book that we update by hand. It's not a perfect system. 
Sometimes  we make  mistakes  because  the information  isn't live, and we accidentally  assign 
a room that's already taken.  
 
 
4. What  do students  complain  about  most  often?  
 
 
They feel left in the dark. After they submit a form, they have no idea what's happening with 
their application  or maintenance  request. This  means  they often  come  to the office  again  and 
again just to ask for updates, which takes up a lot of our time.  
 
 
5. What  would  be the most  helpful  feature  in a new digital  system?  
 
 
A live dashboard showing everything at a glance, which rooms are free, how many 
applications  are waiting,  and what  needs  fixing.  Also,  if the system  could  automatically 
update students on th eir request status, that would save us from answering the same 
questions repeatedly.  
===PAGE===
102  6. How do you handle  maintenance  reports  now,  and what's  not working?  
 
 
We've tried using Google Forms, but it still has problems. Students sometimes don't give 
enough  details  about  the issue  or their room  number.  The reports  also don't  always  reach 
the right person quickly, and it's easy to lose track of which requests are still pending.
